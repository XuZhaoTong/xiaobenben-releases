XiaoBenBen guides

Learn what the app is, how it reads a roster, how Win+G works, and the limits it deliberately keeps.

Updated: 2026-07-10

What is XiaoBenBen?

XiaoBenBen is a local Windows player-notes utility that helps League of Legends players remember people from real matches and the private notes they wrote about them.

→

How does XiaoBenBen record players?

While running, the app reads the roster exposed by the local League Client and Live Client, excludes you and bots, then stores stable player identifiers together with your notes in a local file.

→

How do I review notes with Win+G?

After installing XiaoBenBen, press Win+G and open its widget to see the other nine players, positive and negative counts, and notes from earlier encounters.

→

Are player notes uploaded?

No. XiaoBenBen has no player-note server, account or telemetry. Riot IDs, matches and your notes stay on your Windows PC.

→

What are XiaoBenBen's limitations?

It supports Windows 10/11 x64 and depends on the local League Client roster. Local records do not sync across PCs, and Windows notifications may be hidden by exclusive fullscreen or Focus mode.
